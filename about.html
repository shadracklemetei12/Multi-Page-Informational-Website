What is Specialty Coffee?

The term "specialty coffee" was first coined by Erna Knutsen in 1974 in an issue of Tea & Coffee Trade Journal. She used it to describe beans of the highest flavor profiles which are produced in special geographic microclimates.

"Specialty coffee can consistently exist because of the dedication of the people who have made it their life's work to continuously make quality their highest priority."
— The Specialty Coffee Association (SCA)

The 100-Point Grading Scale

According to the SCA, coffee beans are graded on a strict 100-point scale by certified "Q Graders." To be stamped as specialty grade, the coffee must score 80 points or above. Standard commercial coffee usually scores anywhere between 60 and 75.

  • 80.0 to 84.99: Very Good
  • 85.0 to 89.99: Excellent
  • 90.0 to 100.0: Outstanding
