The Art of the Pour-Over

Pour-over brewing highlights the clean, intricate, and subtle flavor notes of single-origin specialty coffees. It allows for complete control over temperature, brew time, and water flow.

What You Need

  • Pour-over dripper (e.g., Hario V60 or Chemex)
  • Paper filter
  • 20 grams of freshly ground coffee (medium-coarse)
  • 320 grams of hot water (approx. 93°C / 200°F)
  • Digital kitchen scale and timer

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Rinse the Filter: Place the paper filter in the dripper and rinse it with hot water. This removes the paper taste and warms up your vessel. Discard the rinse water.
  2. Add Coffee: Add your 20g of ground coffee into the filter, tap gently to level the bed, and place the setup onto your scale. Tare the scale to zero.
  3. The Bloom: Start your timer. Pour roughly 40g of water over the grounds, ensuring all coffee is wet. Let it sit for 30–45 seconds. You will see bubbles form; this is gases escaping the coffee.
  4. The Main Pour: In a slow, circular motion, gradually pour the remaining water until the scale reaches 320g. Avoid pouring directly onto the paper filter walls.
  5. Enjoy: Let all the water drip through. The total brew time should land right around 3 minutes. Swirl and enjoy!
